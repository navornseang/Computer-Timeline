Ram Timeline

  • 1990 FPM-Ram

    .1990: Introduced as an improvement over standard DRAM.
    .FPM allowed faster access to data within the same row by eliminating the
    need for repeated row address inputs.
    .It's Widely used in early PCs until the mid-1990s.

  • 1994 EDO-Ram

    *1994: EDO DRAM was developed as a successor to FPM.
    *Enhanced performance by allowing the next memory address
    to be accessed while reading the current one.
    *It's popular in computers during the late 1990s.

  • 1996 SD-RAM

    *1996: SDRAM became mainstream in PCs.
    *Unlike earlier DRAMs, SDRAM synchronized with the CPU clock,
    improving speed and efficiency.
    *It's widely used from the late 1990s until it was replaced by DDR SDRAM.

  • 1998 RD-RAM

    *1999: Developed by Rambus Inc., RDRAM was designed for high-speed data transfer.
    *Initially adopted in Intel's high-end Pentium 4 systems but was soon replaced
    due to high costs and heat issues.

  • 2000 DDR-Ram

    *2000: DDR SDRAM was introduced.
    *Widely used in PCs during the early 2000s.

  • 2003 DDR2-Ram

    *2003: DDR2 was introduced as an improvement over DDR SDRAM.
    *Doubled the data rate of DDR by using improved signaling and memory architecture.
    *Became standard in most computers from 2005 to around 2009.

  • 2007 DDR3-Ram

    *2007: DDR3 was launched as a further evolution.
    *Increased speeds (up to 1066 MHz with effective rates of 2133 MT/s).
    *Supported larger memory capacities (up to 16GB per module).

  • 2012 DDR4-Ram

    *2014: DDR4 was introduced.
    *Speeds ranged from 1600 MHz to 3200 MHz (effective rates of 3200 MT/s to 6400 MT/s).
    *Became the standard memory in most devices from 2015 onwards.
