Timelines of Computer Processor↓

  • In 1950s The UNIVAC 1103, The frist CPU was developed for the UNIVAC I computer. It was large and slow, and used vacuum tubes.
  • In 1960s Transistor-based CPUs were developed, which were smaller and more efficient than vacuum tube-based CPUs.
  • In 1971 Intel intruduced the Intel 4004, The first microprocessor. It powered the Busicom calculator.
  • In 1972 Intel introduced the 8008 processor.
  • In 1974 The Intel 8080 become a standard in the computer industry. It was an 8-bit CPU that was used in the MITS Altair 8800.
  • In 1979 The Intel 8088 was released and the Motorola 68000 was released. The Motorola 68000 was used in the Apple Macintosh and Amiga Computers.
  • In 1993 The original Pentium was introduced. This was a departure from the 286/386/486 processor numbers.
  • In 1995 The Pentium Pro was released, This processor was designed for 32-bit server and workstation applications.
  • In 2017 The Core i9-7920X was released, This was Intel's first dektop processor with 12 cores.
  • In 2017 The Ryzen Threadripper 1950X was released too. This was AMD's first processor with 16 cores.
